Tony DiPasquale

Tony is a San Francisco, California-based photographer, filmmaker, and producer specializing in run and trail. He loves telling stories both human and brand.

Services

  • Brand Lifestyle: Photos and videos highlighting your brand's story for your next product launch or big campaign.
  • Athlete Following: Photos and videos of your athletes taking on a big race or FKT for live updates, recaps, or longer storytelling.
  • Race Leader Following: Photos and videos of your race's men or women leaders for live updates or recaps.
  • Athlete Vignettes: Stories highlighting one of your athletes and your brand.
  • Event Coverage: Photos and videos to show off your event or brand activation.

Why Hire Tony?

He's a one-stop-shop. Tony works directly with your teams to make your vision and campaign come to life. He can do everything from ideating the creative to organizing and planning the day, locations, talent, etc. He passionate about creativity and loves taking photos, filming, and editing it all together into a engaging story. He also has a deep network of talented creatives to help when needed. As a passionate trail runner himself, he knows the sport, he knows the people, and he can sympathize with their journey. His passion for his craft shows in the work. He's also fun to work with. Tony is light hearted and loves people. You'll be fast friends.

Let's go deeper.

In 2012, Tony was lost. No, not physically lost. Tony was living in Boston, had just graduated college with a fancy engineering degree, but he was lost. It was around that time that he joined a free fitness group called November Project. Working out with that group, he discovered the power of transformation through a positive and supportive, fitness-based community. Ever since, he's dedicated part of his life to building and fostering, positive and supportive fitness-based communities.

In 2018, Tony was lost. Tony was living in San Francisco after moving there for work. He was a software developer working in the Tech industry getting paid a Tech salary, but he was lost. Finding himself on the other end of an identity shattering breakup, he went on a journey of self-discovery. There, he discovered the power of transformation within himself as well as his thirst for creative, meaningful work. He picked up a camera, waved goodbye to the cushy Tech job, and started building the skills and business you see here today.

Community, fitness, and transformation have been embedded in him and his work as a storyteller. He uses photos and films to tell the stories of communities that are building better spaces for genuine, transformative connection. He uses photos and films to tell the stories of individuals that are building better versions of themselves. He knows first hand the power of these and he's dedicated his work spreading these ideas to everyone through media.
