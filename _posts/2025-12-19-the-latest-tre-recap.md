<p>I’m a little late in my TRE, The Running Event, recap this year. Partly because I got sick from TRE like everyone else, and partly because I had some other things to work on ASAP. Being late isn’t all that bad, though. On the plus side, I read what everyone else thought, and now I can write something different.</p><h2>New City, New Vibe</h2><p class="not-prose my-8 text-center"><a class="inline-flex items-center gap-2 px-5 py-3 rounded bg-gray-900 text-white font-sans font-bold no-underline hover:bg-gray-700" href="https://tonywithasony.substack.com/p/the-latest-tre-recap" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">► Watch this video on Substack</a></p><p>I’ve only been going to TRE for 3 years, with this being my 4th. Perhaps the city has changed in the past, but this was my first time with a city change. The past few years, it’s been in Austin, and this year it was in San Antonio. Austin is a blast. The live music scene, the unique and creative bars, and the general going-out vibes are stellar. San Antonio was pretty damn fun, but the vibe just wasn’t there. Or at least I couldn’t find it.</p><p>Now, of course, this conference is an actual trade show, and it’s not just about going out and the parties, but that definitely is a big reason I love it, and where I find it fun to meet new people and “network” but really just meeting good people and having fun. When people are at their booths on the conference floor, they are focused on their job. But at night, that falls away, and everyone is excited to socialize. And so, I believe it’s important to have good places and vibes to socialize in.</p><p>While Austin is just better set up for this, I think a large part is just everyone trying to figure out this city and what’s good and what’s not good. In a few years, I’m sure we’ll have figured it out. We have at least 4 more years in San Antonio. I will say, the River Walk was pretty cool and definitely the highlight of San Antonio.</p><h2>The Show</h2><p>The conference center is much bigger and more modern than the one we had in Austin, which was definitely noticeable. The floor and the booths themselves all felt pretty similar, though. Some brands keep it simple and show off their latest products, while other brands try to make a splash and stand out. The best booth design, hands down, goes to Tantrums for the second year in a row.</p><figure><img src="https://ik.imagekit.io/tony/blog/the-latest-tre-recap/0152315a-1dad-462f-84c5-9820fefcbacd_5712x4284.jpeg?tr=w-1456" alt="The Latest TRE Recap" loading="lazy"></figure><p>Adam and his team created a bodega with so much detail, I can’t even name it all. Everything in the store was Tantrums branded and there was even a classic security video camera; it was just perfection. Nice work!</p><p>I also liked The North Face booth, where they recreated a bit of the outdoors and had some nice nature sounds playing. I liked it as a contrast to the more industrial booth designs, and it seemed to fit their brand really well.</p><figure><img src="https://ik.imagekit.io/tony/blog/the-latest-tre-recap/c17601d8-99f9-4513-9c42-134d127e1bf5_5712x4284.jpeg?tr=w-1456" alt="The Latest TRE Recap" loading="lazy"></figure><p>Satisfy did their usual closed-off, exclusive space, and Soar did a similar thing on a much smaller scale.</p><figure class="not-prose my-6"><div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-2"><img src="https://ik.imagekit.io/tony/blog/the-latest-tre-recap/f38482b8-089b-4628-996f-46e7ecfa4d7c_5712x4284.jpeg?tr=w-900" alt="" loading="lazy"><img src="https://ik.imagekit.io/tony/blog/the-latest-tre-recap/9d95be54-3ff8-416e-8794-fbcd9998d960_5712x4284.jpeg?tr=w-900" alt="" loading="lazy"></div></figure><p>I actually had to work all day Wednesday, which is the biggest day to roam the conference floor, so I missed out on a lot of things, I’m sure. I did a speed run on Thursday to make up for it. I’m sure there were many other cool booths, but the ones that caught my eye were SuperFeet, Nike, and Altra.</p><figure class="not-prose my-6"><div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-2"><img src="https://ik.imagekit.io/tony/blog/the-latest-tre-recap/806a3c0f-79b6-48c9-a7f0-2b8dcf3dc3a4_5712x4284.jpeg?tr=w-900" alt="" loading="lazy"><img src="https://ik.imagekit.io/tony/blog/the-latest-tre-recap/73e31749-0026-4fcd-b7bf-f473f764b244_5712x4284.jpeg?tr=w-900" alt="" loading="lazy"><img src="https://ik.imagekit.io/tony/blog/the-latest-tre-recap/5335206d-8722-46a4-8030-a3fec6033553_5712x4284.jpeg?tr=w-900" alt="" loading="lazy"><img src="https://ik.imagekit.io/tony/blog/the-latest-tre-recap/af894c1f-e17c-42ae-abc3-d871a53b6613_5712x4284.jpeg?tr=w-900" alt="" loading="lazy"><img src="https://ik.imagekit.io/tony/blog/the-latest-tre-recap/328b9d4e-fded-432f-96d1-5ffa93dc7692_5712x4284.jpeg?tr=w-900" alt="" loading="lazy"></div></figure><p>Altra’s booth design wasn’t much different than last year, but they have continued to rebrand and find their place in this giant, saturated sea of running brands. Their new direction seems to lean into some edginess with the “AF” tagline in most of their wording. From what I’m told, it’s been doing better with the younger crowds. I actually like it personally, too. It’s different than what I saw anywhere else. It feels like a giant shift from their usual brand imagery, but I think it actually does align with their ethos of being different. When everyone was going big cushion, they went zero drop. Whether that’s your thing or not, I think they are a bit unique and radical for continuing to push that. “Unique AF*” they might say.</p><h2>The Brands</h2><p>I read a few posts about no one innovating and everything feeling similar. I get that. There doesn’t feel like there is a huge standout innovation. Maybe that’s because the industry is slowing, maybe it’s because they are hiding all the good stuff now that the show has grown to include too much media. Either way, I don’t mind it. If I think back on the big innovations we’ve had in the past ten years, it’s been super foam manufacturability and carbon plates in road shoes. That’s about it. We don’t need to and probably can’t innovate every year. Small incremental steps seem like a better way. And sure, every now and then, throw in a crazy innovation.</p><p>I thought ON’s LightSpray was going to be the next innovation, but it hasn’t seemed to catch on, and no one is trying to replicate it. I think what Nike is doing with that weird-looking cooling shirt, Radical AirFlow, is super interesting, and I wonder how that will grow and influence other brands. It was also interesting to see Nike leaning so hard into the ACG brand. They have signed a bunch of great athletes and are sponsoring some of my favorite races in 2026. They’re coming back in hard after that hard pull out of Specialty Run they did a few years ago. I hope they’re in it for the long haul. Excited to see how they keep investing.</p><h2>The People</h2><p>My favorite part of TRE is, of course, the people. I love going because people aren’t working in the same way they do at races and events throughout the year. It really feels like a fun chance to connect and chat with all the people I saw this year in a non-pressured situation.</p><p>I had a lot of conversations, and my favorites were with a few peers who all want the same thing. <strong>More community, better storytelling, higher quality content, and deeper connection.</strong> We’re <s>probably</s> biased, I’m sure, but it is really exciting to talk to people who are looking for the same things and trying to build those things. This conference is 95% about gear, but I think that’s the least interesting part of it. I love the planning and scheming. The hoping and dreaming. Of a better running industry and one we’re all more excited to be in.</p><p>Is anyone else feeling this?</p><p></p>
